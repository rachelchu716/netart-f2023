when my older brother and i were kids, dad had tried to teach us how to fold
dumplings the traditional way, but the store-bought dumpling skins were dusted in
more flour than the ones my parents folded as kids, so the crimps simply wouldn't
crimp.

mom instead taught us to fold dumplings by running a wet finger along the
circumference of the interior of the dumpling, folding the circular skin upwards,
(hamburger-style), and pinching the edges together. the wet finger turned the
flour and water mixture into a paste that glued the edges shut.
